## General Online Safety

- [ ] **Use strong, unique passwords** for every account. Use a combination of letters,
      numbers, and symbols. Never reuse the same password across platforms.

- [ ] **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)** on all important accounts – email,
      social media, and banking apps. This adds a second layer of protection even if
      your password is stolen.

- [ ] **Keep your devices and apps updated** – software updates patch known security
      vulnerabilities. Never delay a security update.

- [ ] **Be cautious of public Wi-Fi** – Avoid accessing banking apps or entering passwords
      on unsecured public Wi-Fi networks (cafes, railway stations, airports). Use mobile
      data instead.

- [ ] **Never click on unknown links** received via SMS, WhatsApp, or email – even if
      they appear to come from a known contact. Verify separately before clicking.

- [ ] **Verify before trusting** – If you receive a call claiming to be from your bank or a
      government office, hang up and call the official number listed on their website.

---

## 💳 Financial / UPI Safety (Mandatory Tips)

- [ ] **Remember: You never need to enter your UPI PIN to RECEIVE money.**
      If anyone asks you to enter your PIN or scan a QR code to "receive" a payment,
      it is a scam. Entering your PIN always SENDS money – never receives it.

- [ ] **Never share your OTP, UPI PIN, CVV, or bank password with anyone** –
      not even with people claiming to be bank employees or customer care agents.
      Legitimate banks never ask for this information over a call or message.

- [ ] **Check the UPI collect request carefully** before approving – read the amount and
      recipient name before tapping "Pay." Fraudsters rely on victims acting quickly
      without checking.

- [ ] **Set a daily UPI transaction limit** in your banking app to minimise potential loss
      if your account is ever compromised.

---

## 📱 Social Media & Identity Safety

- [ ] **Do not post photos of sensitive documents** (Aadhaar, PAN card, marksheets,
      fee receipts) on social media, even "just for memories."

- [ ] **Review app permissions regularly** – Revoke access for apps that do not need
      your camera, microphone, contacts, or location.

---

## 🆘 Reporting Channels

If you are a victim of cybercrime or suspect fraud:

| Channel | Details |
|---|---|
| **National Cyber Crime Portal** | [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in) – File a complaint online 24/7 |
| **Cyber Crime Helpline** | **1930** – Available 24x7 for immediate financial fraud reporting |
| **Local Police** | File an FIR at your nearest police station |

> ⚠️ **Act fast** – For UPI/financial fraud, call **1930** immediately. Banks can sometimes
> freeze the fraudster's account if reported within hours.
