# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

**CSE0001 Digital Literacy | Task 4 | SARIN KUMAR | 25BEC10072**

---

## ✅ DO's

1. **Keep your profile professional** – Use a clear, appropriate profile photo and a real name. Future employers and internship coordinators will search for you online.

2. **Think before you post** – Ask yourself: would I be comfortable if my professor, parent, or a future employer saw this? If not, do not post it.

3. **Use privacy settings** – Regularly review who can see your posts, personal details, and tagged photos. Limit personal information to trusted connections.

4. **Share educational and constructive content** – Post about your academic achievements, projects, certifications, and skills. Let your social media reflect your growth.

5. **Give credit when sharing** – Always tag or mention original creators when reposting content, images, or ideas. Plagiarism applies online too.

6. **Report harassment and abuse** – Use platform reporting tools to flag cyberbullying, hate speech, or suspicious accounts. Do not respond aggressively.

7. **Log out on shared devices** – Always sign out of social media accounts when using a shared or public computer or phone.

---

## ❌ DON'Ts

1. **Don't share personal information publicly** – Avoid posting your phone number, home address, Aadhaar/ID details, bank information, or daily routine (e.g., "leaving for college now").

2. **Don't post under the influence of strong emotions** – Angry, impulsive posts can damage friendships, academic standing, and your professional reputation permanently.

3. **Don't engage with or forward unverified information** – Sharing fake news or unverified claims, even if done casually, can have legal consequences and spread harm.

4. **Don't accept friend/follow requests from strangers** – Unknown accounts may be fake profiles used for phishing, scams, or social engineering.

5. **Don't use social media to share exam questions, papers, or internal college documents** – This violates academic integrity policies and can lead to disciplinary action.

6. **Don't overshare location in real time** – Checking in to every location or sharing live location on public stories is a safety risk.

7. **Don't badmouth peers, faculty, or institutions** – Negative comments about your college or professors can have serious consequences and reflect poorly on you long after graduation.

---

*Remember: The internet has a long memory. What you post today may be seen years from now.*
