## What Is UPI Payment Fraud?

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a type of online financial crime in which
fraudsters trick individuals into authorising payments or revealing sensitive credentials,
resulting in unauthorised money transfers. It is one of the most common cybercrimes
targeting students and young adults in India today.

---

## How It Typically Happens – Step by Step

1. **Initial Contact:** The fraudster contacts the victim via phone call, WhatsApp, or SMS,
   posing as a bank official, customer care representative, or even a fellow student.

2. **Creating Urgency:** The scammer claims there is an urgent problem – for example,
   "Your UPI ID is about to be blocked," or "You have received a cashback of ₹500 – click
   here to claim it."

3. **The Trap:** The victim is sent a UPI collect request or a QR code and is told to
   "approve" or "scan" it to receive money. In reality, approving a collect request
   SENDS money – it does not receive it.

4. **Credential Theft:** In some variants, the fraudster asks the victim to share their UPI
   PIN, OTP, or install a remote access app (like AnyDesk or TeamViewer) under the
   guise of "helping" them.

5. **Loss:** Once the PIN or OTP is shared, the fraudster drains the bank account within
   minutes. Transactions are often irreversible.

---

## Who Is Typically Targeted?

- First-year college students unfamiliar with UPI mechanics
- People selling items on OLX, Quikr, or Facebook Marketplace
- Job seekers who receive fake "registration fee refund" calls
- Anyone who has recently posted a complaint on social media about a bank or service

---

## Consequences

- Immediate financial loss, sometimes of the entire bank balance
- Emotional distress, anxiety, and loss of trust in digital payments
- Legal complexity in recovering funds (especially once transferred abroad)
- Risk of identity theft if personal documents were also shared
